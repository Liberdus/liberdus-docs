# Toll

### 1. What is a Toll in Liberdus?
A Toll is a small sender-paid fee required when messaging someone who has not added you as a Friend or Connection. It protects users from spam and ensures messages are meaningful.

### 2. Why does Liberdus use Tolls instead of filtering spam like other apps?
Traditional platforms allow free messaging, which invites mass spam. Liberdus adds an economic cost to unsolicited messages, making spam expensive and protecting users. It also lets you monetize your attention instead of the platform.

### 3. When do I have to pay a Toll?
You only pay a Toll if the recipient has set you as Tolled, or if they haven't added you as a Friend or Connection.

### 4. What if I added someone as a Friend, but they didnâ€™t add me back?
You still must pay the Toll until they add you as a Friend or Connection. Your settings only affect their messages to you; not the other way around.

### 5. How much is the minimum Toll?
The minimum toll is $0.10 USD. You cannot set a lower toll amount. However, you can disable tolls entirely by changing the toll amount to $0 USD.

### 6. How is the toll amount paid? What currency is used?
You enter the Toll value in USD, and Liberdus automatically converts it into LIB at the current market price.

### 8. Can I turn tolls off completely?
Yes. Setting your toll to 0 USD disables tolls and lets anyone message you freely.

### 9. What happens when someone pays a Toll to message me?
When you read the message, you receive 50% of the toll. When you reply, you receive the remaining 50%.

### 11. What if I never read the message?
If you ignore it completely for 7 days, the sender gets a full refund.

### 12. How does reclaiming the toll work?
After 7 days of no reply or no read, the sender just needs to reopen the chat, and the refund happens automatically.
